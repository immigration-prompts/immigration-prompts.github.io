# Instructions (prompt) for AI – Summary of Original Contribution
1. Upload information about the beneficiary's original contribution. This could be a journal article, a patent, a technical doc, or other information.
2. Copy/paste the following into your AI input. Word count will depend on desired letter length. It is helpful to have summaries of different lengths to use as an overview, part of the support/cover letter, part of a recommendation letter, or in different sections of support letters (e.g., original contributions, authored publications, critical/essential/leading role/capacity)
3. Note regarding guiding AI about the intended audience: second year of college, non-technical seems to be a sweet spot. AI's tone for a high school graduate can come across as "Hey check this out!" Tone for a college graduate can be overly complex, wordy, and jargon-filled. Try a few different approaches -- this is my current iteration for Claude's latest (2025-2026) models, but will likely change as models evolve. 


## The AI prompt

Would you summarize this for a non-technical audience (second year of college)? Focus on achievement and impact on the field. Include any notable statistics or metrics, especially improvements over existing methods and use by others. Please prepare 3 versions: 100-150 words; 250-300 words; and 500-600 words. Suggest if you think a different approach is better. Any questions?
